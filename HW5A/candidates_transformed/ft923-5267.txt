US INSURERS expect to pay out an estimated Dollars 7.3 bn -LRB- Pounds 3.7 bn -RRB- in Florida as a result of Hurricane Andrew - by far the costliest disaster the industry has ever faced .
The figure is the first official tally of the damage resulting from the hurricane , which ripped through southern Florida last week .
In the battered region it is estimated that 275,000 people still have no electricity and at least 150,000 are either homeless or are living amid ruins .
President George Bush yesterday made his second visit to the region since the hurricane hit .
